We the People first. Always.

Who am I?

I’m Kyle Xu — born and raised in Worcester, Massachusetts. I know what it’s like to come from the bottom and fight for every opportunity. My parents struggled with cocaine addiction and were never really in the picture. My sister stepped up to raise me, teaching me that family is about more than just blood; it’s about taking care of each other. I will do everything possible to prove to you that I consider you family.
Those early experiences shaped my belief that everyone deserves a fair shot, no matter where they start. I worked my way through school, eventually earning a degree in psychology, determined to understand and help others facing the same struggles I saw growing up.
At 36, I’m old enough to know how the system works — and young enough to still believe I can help change it.


My Public Service and Experience

Since my junior year of high school, I’ve volunteered for Democratic leaders who put people first — from Mayor Thomas Menino to Senator John Kerry. Those campaigns taught me that progress doesn’t come from the top down — it comes from neighbors talking to neighbors, standing together for something better.

Over the years, I’ve worked with local nonprofits and community outreach programs to support families dealing with addiction, housing insecurity, and mental health challenges.


Why I’m Running

I’m running because I know what it feels like to be left behind. I’m tired of watching working families get ignored while corporations and lobbyists slowly encroach on our personal freedoms and not caring enough to pay a livable wage.

As your representative, I’ll fight for:
Affordable healthcare and addiction recovery support no matter the income or background. No questions asked.
Strengthening public schooling and after-school programs that enable every child.
Livable working wages and hours so working families can not only live without fear of putting food on the table but also get a chance to spend time with the ones they love.
